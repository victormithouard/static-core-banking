Research Note: The "Reseller" Definition

In the composable SaaS market, traditional "Software Reselling" is rare. Instead, Mambu utilizes an Ecosystem Partner Model. "OEM" in this context refers to Managed Service Providers (MSPs) or Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS) providers who embed Mambu as their white-labeled engine, or System Integrators (SIs) who hold the commercial paper in a prime-contractor relationship.

Strategic Recommendation

For Enterprise Banks: The "Reseller" path is typically a prime contract with a GSI (Accenture, Deloitte, Capgemini). These firms have dedicated Mambu centers of excellence and can resell the license as part of a multi-year transformation deal, offering risk mitigation and scale.

For Neobanks & Fintechs: Look towards Specialist SIs (Endava, Mobiquity, Reply). These partners operate with "Agile Pods" and are often faster to deploy "Greenfield" Mambu instances. They are more likely to act as a pseudo-OEM by building bespoke IP on top of the engine.

For Non-Banks (Embedded Finance): Consider BaaS Platforms. Companies like Solaris (often a partner/competitor hybrid) or vertical-specific engines provide the license, the regulatory umbrella, and the tech stack in one contract.

⚙

Build vs. Buy vs. Partner

Most "OEM" deals in Europe are actually "Managed Service" agreements. Ensure your contract specifies who owns the configuration IP.
